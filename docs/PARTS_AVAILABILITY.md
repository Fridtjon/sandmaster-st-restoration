# Parts Availability Research

Research on sourcing parts for the 1994 Kyosho Sandmaster ST.

## Current Status

Original Kyosho parts for the Sandmaster ST are **discontinued**. The model is now vintage/collector status. However, parts are still obtainable through various channels.

**Important:** The modern "Kyosho Sandmaster" (electric buggy) is a completely different vehicle. Those parts are NOT compatible with your 1994 ST nitro truck.

## GS11X Engine Parts

The Kyosho GS11X is a rebadged HPI 12E engine. HPI parts may be cross-compatible.

## Critical Wear Items

**Replace these regardless of visual condition after 30 years:**

### Engine
- [ ] Glow plug (ProTek N3 Hot Standard recommended)
- [ ] Head gasket
- [ ] Crankcase seals
- [ ] Carburetor o-rings and springs
- [ ] One-way bearing (if equipped)

### Fuel System
- [ ] All fuel lines (silicone perishes)
- [ ] Fuel filter
- [ ] Tank stopper o-rings
- [ ] Primer bulb (if equipped)

### Chassis
- [ ] All rubber o-rings
- [ ] Shock seals and bladders
- [ ] Shock oil (replace with fresh)
- [ ] All bearings (inspect, replace if rough)
- [ ] Wheel hex hubs (if worn)

### Drivetrain
- [ ] Clutch shoes and springs
- [ ] Spur gear (inspect teeth)
- [ ] Pinion gear (inspect teeth)
- [ ] Drive belts (if equipped)

## Recommended Actions

### Immediate
1. **Join RC10Talk.com** - Create account, introduce yourself and project
2. **Set up eBay alerts** for:
   - "Kyosho Sandmaster ST"
   - "Kyosho GS11X"
   - "Kyosho 1/10 nitro truck"
3. **Document your specific needs** before contacting sellers

### Before Ordering Parts
1. Fully disassemble and assess truck
2. List all broken/missing/worn parts with part numbers if possible
3. Check manual for part numbers
4. Cross-reference with forums for alternatives

### Engine Priority
If your GS11X engine is intact:
1. Drain any old fuel immediately
2. Apply after-run oil through glow plug hole and carburetor
3. Turn over by hand to distribute oil
4. Test if it turns freely (not seized)
5. Assess compression before buying parts

## Conclusion

Restoration is **absolutely feasible** with patience. The vintage RC community is active and supportive. Main challenges:
- Engine-specific internal parts
- Body shells (rare)
- Certain chassis-specific plastic parts

These can be overcome through forums, eBay patience, and creative solutions (3D printing, fabrication, compatible alternatives).
